Chollam (சோளம்) Sorghum millet

Sorghum millet is a gluten-free grain rich in fiber, protein, iron, and calcium. Its low glycemic index helps control blood sugar, making it ideal for diabetics. Drought-resistant and sustainable, it’s commonly used in porridges and traditional dishes, supporting digestive health and weight management.

Thinai (தினை) Foxtail Millet

Foxtail millet is a nutritious, gluten-free grain high in fiber and protein. It supports digestive health and has a low glycemic index, making it suitable for diabetics. Additionally, it is drought-resistant and can thrive in arid climates.

Ragi (ராகி) Finger millet

Finger millet, also known as ragi, is a nutrient-rich grain high in calcium, fiber, and antioxidants. It has a low glycemic index, making it beneficial for blood sugar control, and is often used in porridge, bread, and various traditional dishes. This drought-resistant crop is easy to cultivate in diverse climates.

Cooking Method

Samai (சாமை) Little millet

Little millet is a small, nutritious grain that is rich in fiber, protein, and essential minerals. Known for its low glycemic index, it helps in managing blood sugar levels. This drought-resistant crop is often used in traditional dishes like porridge and is a great alternative to rice and wheat, supporting digestive health and overall wellness.

Cooking Method

Kambu (கம்பு) Pearl millet

Pearl millet is a highly nutritious, gluten-free grain rich in fiber, protein, and essential minerals like iron and magnesium. It has a low glycemic index, making it beneficial for blood sugar control. Commonly used in traditional dishes and flatbreads, pearl millet is drought-resistant and thrives in arid conditions, making it a sustainable crop option.
